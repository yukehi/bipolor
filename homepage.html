what is bipolor disorder

is a mental disorder characterized by periods of depression and periods of abnormally elevated mood that last from days to weeks each. If the elevated mood is severe or associated with psychosis, it is called mania; if it is less severe, it is called hypomania. During mania, an individual behaves or feels abnormally energetic, happy, or irritable,and they often make impulsive decisions with little regard for the consequences. There is usually also a reduced need for sleep during manic phases. During periods of depression, the individual may experience crying and have a negative outlook on life and poor eye contact with others. The risk of suicide is high

depression effects

  • Long-lasting sadness or irritability.
  • Extremely high and low moods.
  • Excessive fear, worry, or anxiety.
  • Social withdrawal.
  • Dramatic changes in eating or sleeping habits.

manic effects

  • Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity.
  • Decreased need for sleep (e.g., feels rested after 3 hours of sleep).
  • More talkative than usual, or acts pressured to keep talking.
  • Flights of ideas or subjective experience that thoughts are racing.
  • Increase in goal directed activity, or psychomotor acceleration.

how can you help

At every stage of bipolar there are signs, the person does not always have the ability to diagnose the objective situation he is in, it is very important to be attentive and empathetic towards the person and try to convey to him that something is wrong with him maybe he did not take his medication. Properly, it is important to understand how frightening it is to be at each stage of the disease and how much it isolates the person from social life and social activity.

in manic stage

At this point a person was very impulsive and manipulative and sometimes childish, his thoughts will run at the speed of light, he changes subject and his desires who moment by moment, it is very important to be attentive and understand if there is aggravation to psychotic state, inability to see objectivity reality and consequences. Can harm him, it is advisable to bring him directly to the nearest hospital for treatment before the condition worsens.

in depression stage

At this point a person will begin to disengage from social contact, will begin to sleep a lot, will cry for no apparent reason, will lock himself inside. It is very important to be in touch, to come and try to get out of the house, to make sure that he does not have suicidal thoughts or self-harm , if there is a suspicion, to call a therapist or the nearest hospital and get treatment urgently.
